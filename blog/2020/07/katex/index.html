KaTeX

by Kevin Barabash

KaTeX is a simple, yet powerful, way of including maths in pages.

Usage

Basically just use $, $$, \begin{...} like you would in LaTeX.

Single $ for inline maths like so: $i := \sqrt{-1}$ which gives: i:=−1i := \sqrt{-1}i:=−1​,

Double $$ for display maths like so: $$ \exp(i\pi) + 1 = 0 $$ which gives:

exp⁡(iπ)+1=0 \exp(i\pi) + 1 = 0 exp(iπ)+1=0

You can also use alignment environments:

\begin{align}
x &= 5 \\
y &= x + 2 \\
z &= y^2 \\
\end{align}

which gives:

x=5y=x+2z=y2\begin{aligned} x &= 5 \\ y &= x + 2 \\ z &= y^2 \\ \end{aligned}xyz​=5=x+2=y2​

or if you want more spacing, use eqnarray:

\begin{eqnarray}
x &=& 5 \\
y &=& x + 2 \\
z &=& y^2 \\
\end{eqnarray}

which gives:

x=5y=x+2z=y2\begin{array}{rcl} x &=& 5 \\ y &=& x + 2 \\ z &=& y^2 \\ \end{array}xyz​===​5x+2y2​

if you don't want a display block to be numbered, you can wrap it in @@no-number ... @@

@@no-number
\begin{eqnarray}
x &=& 5 \\
y &=& x + 2 \\
z &=& y^2 \\
\end{eqnarray}
@@

which gives:

x=5y=x+2z=y2\begin{array}{rcl} x &=& 5 \\ y &=& x + 2 \\ z &=& y^2 \\ \end{array}xyz​===​5x+2y2​
